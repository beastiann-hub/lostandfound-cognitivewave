You Fly Alone

BASTART

A confrontation with the darkness that lives within. This song emerges from the depths of depression—not as surrender, but as testimony. Through layers of acoustic guitar, delay, and reverb, it maps the territory of isolation and the slow, difficult journey toward something that might resemble flight.

This work explores themes of depression, suicidal ideation, and profound isolation. It is intended as artistic expression and connection for those who understand these depths.

Artist Statement

Depression is not a metaphor. It is not romantic. It is a disease that kills, slowly and thoroughly, erasing the self from the inside out. This song was written in that space—where breath becomes labor and existence feels like an elaborate cruelty.

But within that truthful acknowledgment of darkness, something else emerges. The ability to name the thing. To give it form through sound. To transform the weight of isolation into something that might reach another person drowning in their own silence.

"You fly alone"—this is both the cruelest truth and the beginning of freedom. When you have nothing left to lose, when you have descended to the bottom of your own psyche, there is a strange liberation. You learn that you can survive in places you never thought possible. You learn to fly with broken wings.
